Overlays Design Pattern Research

February 07, 2023

The article, "Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows” by Naema Baskanderi emphasize the idea and usage for Modals, Overlays, Dialog Windows in a webpage. According to the article, Modals, Overlays, Dialog Windows are described as the element and feature in a page or application that will pop out in the foreground of the screen, over the main window, disabling the software but maintains it visible in the background. Said window was meant to capture user's attention to give out important warnings, signs, and any other information, that the user's must interact with or

need to know to continue on with their activity. To sum up, the general purpose of implementing Modals, Overlays, Dialog Windows is for user's to focus for something in any program. To achieve that, the article mentioned some of the best practices for applying the layout for the Modals, Overlays, Dialog Windows. The anatomy of said windows may need an escape hatch: to get out of it, a descriptive title: to provide context, and button: for options and actions. Ultimately, It should be limited in scope since material should fit within it or a separate tab may be necessary for a significant amount of data, employ lightbox

effect and darken background and it should triggered by users. In conclusion, I think the article was pretty helpful to keep in mind and to be aware for the UI/UX of webpage and application designs or layout. It seems that Modals, Overlays, Dialog Windows have significant advantages, including engaging the user's attentiveness, conserving concepts or details, and simplifying the user interface. Overall, I learned that some applying Modals, Overlays, Dialog Windows is to make sure that user's are not overwhelmed and create understanding to the context into websites and mobile apps.

Research Form Design

January 31, 2023

The article, "Best Practices For Form Design” by Salim Ansari explains tips and tricks on how a design layout for a form webpage can be improved and engaging to the users. From what was mentioned, the key idea for developing a structure for a form page is the conversion from its UI/UX, employing specific elements that were further described in the article. To summarize, it seems that the webpage must be interactive and communicative to the users, where it is simple to understand for the audience to fill out the form with ease and make them commit until the end of it. The form should not overwhelm the

users, the format needs to be concise and direct wherein there small questions or the informations are broken down through multiple steps to avoid much scrolling, as the objective here is to make it less than more. Ultimately, it also important to provide explanations and distinguish guidelines like labels, field size, button options, placeholder texts, and such for the users to know what it is needed to be done, making it as accessible as possible for anyone. From what I learned, what is critical to do is to make the page as universal or feasible ever. I think a great example could be the "Walgreens” form for

appointments, as the setup has intstructions and descriptions that also gives minimal fields where it expands as I go, making it fast and easy to answer properly. Overall, I believe the article had a valuable case and useful informations. As technology evolves so should the designing to match the credentials for individuals needs and satisfication, and the techniques and strategies should be considered and to be applied for. Finally, I think the essential element to keep in mind is the attention to detailing a webpage.
