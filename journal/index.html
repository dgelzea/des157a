Research Form Design

January 31, 2023

The article, "Best Practices For Form Design” by Salim Ansari explains tips and tricks on how a design layout for a form webpage can be improved and engaging to the users. From what was mentioned, the key idea for developing a structure for a form page is the conversion from its UI/UX, employing specific elements that were further described in the article.

To summarize, it seems that the webpage must be interactive and communicative to the users, where it is simple to understand for the audience to fill out the form with ease and make them commit until the end of it. The form should not overwhelm the users, the format needs to be concise and direct wherein there small questions or the informations are broken down through multiple steps to avoid much scrolling, as the objective here is to make it less than more. Ultimately, it also important to provide explanations and distinguish guidelines like labels, field size, button options, placeholder texts, and such for the users to know what it is needed to be done, making it as accessible as possible for anyone.

From what I learned, what is critical to do is to make the page as universal or feasible ever. I think a great example could be the "Walgreens” form for appointments, as the setup has intstructions and descriptions that also gives minimal fields where it expands as I go, making it fast and easy to answer properly. Overall, I believe the article had a valuable case and useful informations. As technology evolves so should the designing to match the credentials for individuals needs and satisfication, and the techniques and strategies should be considered and to be applied for. Finally, I think the essential element to keep in mind is the attention to detailing a webpage.
